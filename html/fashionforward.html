Turning Waste into Fashion: How Youth Redesigned the Future in Krosno

Fashion Forward: Recycled Runway

Back

Subjects

Sustainable Fashion, Digital Innovation

Where

Krosno, Poland

When

29 March – 6 April 2024

Description

From March 29 to April 6, 2024, Krosno became the stage for innovation, creativity, and climate action through Fashion Forward: Recycled Runway, an Erasmus+ Youth Exchange organized by Fundacja Patrz Globalnie.
The project united 28 participants from Poland, Portugal, North Macedonia, and Hungary to tackle one of today’s biggest challenges: the destructive impact of the fast fashion industry.

Our mission was to bold—transform waste into fashion, inspire sustainable choices, and harness the power of technology to amplify change.
More than a typical exchange, Fashion Forward combined AI-powered creativity, hands-on upcycling, and intercultural learning , giving participants the tools to rethink fashion and advocate for a greener future.

One of the project’s most eye-opening experiences was our visit to MPGK Krosno, the city’s waste management and recycling facility. There, we explored the entire recycling chain, from sorting materials to advanced water purification systems, and learned how the city uses fermentation to produce biogas and generate electricity. At the clothes section of the recycling facility, we saw how everyday choices and the fast fashion industry impact the environment and discovered practical circular economy solutions in action.
For many, it was a powerful reminder that sustainability begins at home, and that every small effort counts, including our choices of clothing and source of materials.


Back at our workspace, creativity took center stage. Armed with trash collected from around the venue, discarded clothing from MPGK, and trash we collected during the project, and tools like glue guns, sewing machines, and wire and more, we set out to create bold fashion statements.
Combining AI design tools like MidJourney with hands-on craftsmanship, we turned waste into unique outfits full of meaning. These weren’t just clothes—they were manifestos for a sustainable future.


To share these messages with the community, we organized the Fashion Forward Catwalk on April 4 at Centrum Aktywności Młodzieży (Youth Centre in Krosno). Before the runway began, guests watched an AI-generated short film, created by participants using Sora AI, KlingAI, FakeYou, and InVideo AI, showcasing dramatic, emotional scenes about the impact of fast fashion on the planet.
The catwalk itself featured stunning, upcycled designs that wowed the audience and sparked conversations about conscious consumerism. This event proved that fashion can be both beautiful and responsible.


Fashion Forward wasn’t only about fashion—it was about people, ideas, and cultural exchange. Through lively discussions on fast fashion, creative solution-building sessions, and intercultural evenings filled with music and food, participants built friendships that crossed borders. These shared moments gave rise to future initiatives like Sustainable Fashion Clubs, ensuring that the impact continues long after the project.

Fashion Forward: Recycled Runway was more than a youth exchange—it was a movement toward responsibility, creativity, and change. We are grateful to everyone who supported us, including local partners who amplified our message and helped us reach the community. Special thanks to:
