Turning Waste into Music: Can Music Save Our Planet

Follow Your Drums!

Back

Subjects

Music, Junk Art

Where

Krosno, Poland

When

17 - 25 Sept 2025

Description

From 17–25 September, we took part in the Erasmus+ youth exchange Follow Your Drums! in Krosno, Poland, together with participants from Portugal, Hungary, Romania, North Macedonia, Poland.
On the first day, we focused on getting to know each other and creating a positive group atmosphere. Very quickly, we felt comfortable, connected, and ready to collaborate. The project presentation was done in a dynamic and interactive way, which immediately motivated us and helped us understand the goals of the exchange.

We explored Erasmus+ opportunities by creating AI-generated videos instead of sitting through a traditional workshop. This allowed us to develop d igital skills while promoting youth exchanges, volunteering, and mobility programs. Later, we went into the city to pick up trash and interview local residents. This action helped us support the environment, but also connect with the local community and raise awareness about sustainability.

Music was the core of the project. With the support of our facilitator and drumming expert Piotr, we transformed collected waste into percussion instruments. We sanitized materials, decorated them, and turned trash into art. Then we formed groups and began composing rhythms and songs, either by covering known tracks or creating our own. It was inspiring to see how much creativity could come from recycled objects.


As the week continued, our teamwork and musical skills grew stronger. We spent time practicing, experimenting, and jamming as a full group. One of the highlights was composing one big group song that we all performed together. We felt like a real band, learning how to listen, synchronize, and support each other. Later, each group recorded their songs using multiple devices, and we mixed the best audio to achieve high-quality sound our first experience with music production.




We also combined creativity with technology by producing music videos using AI tools such as Viddo.ai and Google Veo3. We merged AI-generated visuals with our own footage, creating modern and artistic results. This activity showed us how innovation and digital tools can enhance artistic expression and make our work more impactful.




One of the most eye-opening moments was the visit to MPGK Krosno, the city’s waste management and water treatment facility. We learned how recycling and resource management work in real life, how AI and robotics support the process, and how clean energy is produced from bio-fermentation and solar farms. We were impressed that this energy is used to power public buses and electric vehicles. Meeting the president of MPGK gave us deeper insight into sustainability on a regional level.

The project concluded with a public concert at the Krosno Youth Centre, where we performed our songs live and even jammed with the audience and local decision-makers. It was a powerful moment to share our message with the community. On the final day, we reflected on everything we learned, received our Youthpass certificates, and celebrated with farewell activities. We returned home with new friendships, confidence, creative skills, and the belief that when we follow our rhythm together, we can inspire real change.
